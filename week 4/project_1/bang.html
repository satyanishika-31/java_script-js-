Three Icons of Bangalore’s Charm

The Serenity of Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Lalbagh Botanical Garden is Bangalore’s green jewel, celebrated for its rare tropical plants, centuries-old trees, and tranquil atmosphere. At its heart lies the iconic Glass House, modeled after London’s Crystal Palace, which hosts dazzling flower shows twice a year. This lush sanctuary offers a peaceful escape from the city’s bustle, making it a favorite for nature lovers, families, and photographers alike.

The Grandeur of Bangalore Palace

Bangalore Palace stands as a regal reminder of the city’s aristocratic past, with its Tudor-style architecture, fortified towers, and sprawling grounds. Inside, visitors encounter ornate furniture, intricate wood carvings, and royal memorabilia from the Wodeyar dynasty. Often compared to Windsor Castle in England, the palace embodies grandeur and elegance, offering history buffs and photographers a glimpse into Bangalore’s royal heritage.

The Wild Spirit of Bannerghatta National Park

Bannerghatta National Park brings adventure and wilderness to Bangalore’s doorstep. Known for its thrilling safaris, the park allows visitors to witness tigers, lions, elephants, and bears in their natural habitats. Beyond the safaris, it features a zoo, butterfly park, and a rescue center for endangered species, making it both an educational and exhilarating experience. Just 22 km from the city, it’s the perfect destination for families and adventure seekers craving a close encounter with nature.
